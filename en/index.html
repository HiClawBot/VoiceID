Protocol

Voice sign-in becomes an identity layer.

VoiceID is not another account entry point. It organizes voice proof, wallet ownership, and decentralized identity into an auditable protocol surface.

Voice research direction

The target is local or trusted-edge feature verification. The Playground only measures duration, volume, peak, and clipping; it does not identify a speaker.

Decentralized identity

Credentials bind to user-controlled wallets and keys instead of a single account provider.

Multi-chain wallet

One voice identity can connect assets, signatures, payments, and recovery across chain ecosystems.

Privacy protection

The verification flow is designed to expose the smallest useful proof to wallets, apps, and web sign-in systems.

Identity Wallet

Protect wallets with cryptographic credentials, then gate voice signals carefully.

The interfaces below are future product concepts. A real Online Beta uses a passkey or server-verified wallet signature as its trust root; voice remains observe-only until model, privacy, and attack gates pass.

  • Local audio-quality checks and data minimization
  • Private keys, PIN fallback, and recovery flows
  • A unified authentication entry point for apps

Developers

A clear integration surface for apps, wallets, and web sign-in.

The site publishes the project structure, protocol direction, and brand baseline first. The code sample illustrates the planned SDK shape; the repository remains the source of truth.

  1. 01 Create a demo proof

    The current client creates a demo-only proof from basic audio-quality measurements.

  2. 02 Bind identity and wallet

    The proof binds to a decentralized identifier, wallet address, and authorization scope.

  3. 03 Sign or sign in

    The user completes sign-in, signatures, transaction approvals, or recovery through VoiceID.
